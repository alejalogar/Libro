Our History

Severo Ochoa was an internationally renowned Spanish physician and scientist, nationalized in the United States in 1956. In 1959, he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine, together with the American Arthur.


Since its first call in 1981, he has served as president of the jury for the Prince of Asturias Award for Sciences. Since 1986, he has devoted himself mainly to giving lectures, attending the media and dealing with students at the Madrid Molecular Biology Center.


In 1993 he presented in Madrid his biography entitled The emotion of discovery and that same year he died at the age of 88.

Another very remarkable point is that it has been recognised by the "Consellería de Educación", as a centre of Excellence in Vocational Training specialising in advanced systems corresponding to the professional family of computing and communications.
